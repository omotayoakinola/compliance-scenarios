# Globacom vs Finastra — Comparative Breach Analysis

## Overview
A comparative analysis of two significant cybersecurity incidents across 
two continents, examining how organizational culture, regulatory maturity, 
and governance structure shape incident response outcomes. This report 
analyses the Globacom DNS hijack attack of 2023 and the Finastra credential 
breach of 2024 across nine analytical dimensions, producing a cross-incident 
remediation roadmap applicable to any organization operating critical 
digital infrastructure.

## Key Findings

**Finding 1 — Governance structure determines response quality**
Globacom's centralized founder-led governance produced concealment and 
institutional paralysis. Finastra's institutional structure produced rapid 
technical containment but failed on consumer transparency. Organizational 
culture, not technical capability, is the primary determinant of incident 
response quality.

**Finding 2 — Regulatory maturity shapes organizational behaviour**
Finastra operated under one of the world's most developed cybersecurity 
frameworks and responded with relative transparency. Globacom operated 
under a newly enacted regulatory framework with limited enforcement 
capacity, creating minimal institutional incentive for disclosure. Mature 
regulation creates a culture of compliance; immature regulation creates 
a culture of concealment.

**Finding 3 — Both incidents share identical technical root causes**
Inadequate access controls, absence of multi-factor authentication, 
insufficient monitoring, and failure to treat cybersecurity as a 
board-level governance priority. The gap between a Nigerian 
telecommunications company and a global British fintech is narrower 
than their regulatory environments suggest when it comes to fundamental 
security hygiene.
